Hi-C Heat Map

Hi-C is a kind of data that records the frequency of interaction between different sites of two chromosomes. The frequency of interaction can express the relationship of chromatin in the three-dimensional space of the nucleus. We can use heat maps to visualize the Hi-C matrix. This will be very helpful for biologists to conduct relevant analysis.

The demo data is human fetal lung fibroblasts Hi-C data (IMR90; SRA: SRR400264) from Dixon, J. R., Selvaraj, S., Yue, F., Kim, A., Li, Y., Shen, Y., Hu, M., Liu, J. S., & Ren, B. (2012). Topological domains in mammalian genomes identified by analysis of chromatin interactions. Nature, 485(7398), 376–380. https://doi.org/10.1038/nature11082
